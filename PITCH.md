# Throughline — Positioning & Pitch

> *The research workflow, end to end. Eight connected tools that carry your work forward.*

This is the written positioning for **Throughline** (the Research Suite as one product). It is the source of truth for how we talk about the suite — to students, instructors, departments, and anyone evaluating it. Companion doc: [ROADMAP.md](./ROADMAP.md).

---

## 1. The one-liner

**Throughline turns the social-science research process into one connected workflow — from the first hunch to a submission-ready manuscript — with methodological rigor built into every handoff.**

Reference tagline: *"Strava for the research process."* You don't just do the work; you can see the whole arc, and each step makes the next one easier.

---

## 2. The problem

A social-science or management researcher today stitches their project together from a dozen disconnected tools:

- a reference manager for the literature,
- a notes app for ideas,
- a stats package (SPSS / JASP / R) for analysis,
- a survey platform for data collection,
- a word processor for writing,
- and a lot of manual copy-paste, re-formatting, and *re-deriving context they already had* at every boundary.

Each tool is good at its slice. **None of them know about each other.** The construct you operationalised in your survey isn't linked to the theory you chose, which isn't linked to the analysis you ran, which isn't linked to the gap you claimed in your intro. The *throughline* of the project — the chain of decisions that makes it coherent — lives only in the researcher's head, and it leaks at every handoff.

This is worst exactly where it matters most: **methods**. Students lose marks, and papers get desk-rejected, not because the idea was bad but because the chain broke — the hypothesis didn't match the design, the scale didn't match the construct, the test didn't match the data.

---

## 3. The solution

Eight tools, one continuous workflow. Each step **carries its output forward** into the next, as structured data — not as a PDF you re-type.

| # | Tool | Phase | What it carries forward |
|---|------|-------|--------------------------|
| 0 | **IdeaBox** | Capture the hunch | a raw idea, tagged |
| 1 | **Wordmap** | Spot a trend / white space | a construct's trend signal → typed gap |
| 2 | **PaperCards** | Read the literature as cards | recurring constructs + a heuristic gap |
| 3 | **ResearchFlow** | Question → theory → design | a full ResearchPack (question, theory, hypotheses, design, sampling) |
| 4 | **TheoryScope** | Pick theories | chosen theory + propositions |
| 5 | **ScaleScope** | Operationalise | validated measurement scales |
| 6 | **Cadence** | Collect data | a live study + responses |
| 7 | **ToolsScope** | Analyze + visualize | Methods + Results paragraphs + figures |
| 8 | **JournalTime** | Target journals + write | a manuscript draft + journal shortlist |

The handoffs are the product. A gap found in PaperCards seeds the idea in ResearchFlow. The scale picked in ScaleScope arrives in Cadence already matched. The study collected in Cadence opens in ToolsScope with the *planned* analyses already highlighted. The analysis written in ToolsScope lands in JournalTime as a Methods + Results section. **The chain holds.**

### What makes it defensible

Each individual tool has strong, free competitors — Research Rabbit, JASP, Qualtrics, Zotero. We do **not** win by out-featuring any one of them. We win on two things they structurally cannot copy:

1. **The connected workflow.** They are points; we are the line. The value compounds across steps, and no single competitor spans the whole arc.
2. **Methodological rigor as a first-class citizen.** Every tool is grounded in real curated knowledge (449 theories, 174 validated scales, 98 trend constructs) and cites its sources. The stats are hand-verified against textbook values. The suite doesn't just let you do research — it nudges you toward defensible research.

## 4. Who it's for (the wedge)

**Primary wedge: research-methods education in social science & management.**

- **Graduate students** running their first independent project — thesis, dissertation, capstone. They feel the broken-chain pain most acutely and have the least money to spend on five separate subscriptions.
- **Methods instructors** who need to *show* the whole research process as one coherent arc — and want students to produce work where the chain is visible and gradeable.

Why this wedge:
- The pain (broken methodological chain) is sharpest here.
- The buyers (departments, instructors) are reachable and concentrated.
- "Rigor built in" is a *feature* in education, where in industry it can read as friction.
- It seeds habit early — today's grad student is tomorrow's PI and tomorrow's reviewer.

**Then widen** to independent researchers, small labs, and applied-research teams in industry (UX research, people analytics, policy evaluation) who run the same arc with the same broken handoffs.

---

## 5. Why now

- AI makes each step cheaper to assist — but un-grounded AI gives confident, generic, sometimes wrong answers. Throughline's grounding (real catalogs + verified stats + citations) is the antidote, and it only works *because* the tools are connected.
- Replication and rigor are under scrutiny across the social sciences. A workflow that makes the decision-chain explicit is timely.
- The cost of building a connected suite has collapsed (a single AI backend powers Generate panels across every tool); the cost of *trusting* an un-grounded one has risen.

---

## 6. What we are not

- **Not another reference manager / stats package / survey tool.** We connect those jobs; we don't try to beat each incumbent on depth.
- **Not a black box.** Stats are transparent and hand-verified; AI suggestions are grounded and cited; grounding is a *starting bias, not a cage* (off-catalog picks are allowed, with a citation).
- **Not (yet) a data processor for sensitive human-subjects data at scale.** See blockers in ROADMAP.

## 7. Proof it's real

Not a deck — a working suite, all deployed:

- 8 tools live across Vercel + GitHub Pages, plus a hub (Throughline) framing them as one product.
- Real cross-tool data handoffs (ResearchPack carrier) wired end to end: ResearchFlow → Cadence → ToolsScope → JournalTime round-trips.
- Catalog grounding live on all three legs (449 theories, 174 scales, 98 trend constructs).
- A "Generate everywhere" entry point on every tool, powered by one shared AI backend.
- All 17 of the author's projects brought to a documented world-class bar (accessibility, SEO, performance, academic credibility).

## 8. The ask / next step

The product *exists*. What it lacks to be *sellable* is identity, persistence, and a price — see [ROADMAP.md](./ROADMAP.md). The near-term goal is a credible pilot with one methods cohort: real students, real projects, end to end, and a written account of where the chain held and where it broke.
